The weight and size of wolves vary greatly throughout the world, tending to increase proportionally with latitude, as predicted by Christian Bergmann's theory. In general, height, measured from the shoulders, varies from 60 to 95 centimeters.

Weight varies geographically. On average, European wolves weigh 38.5 kg (84 lb); North American wolves, 36 kg (70 lb); Indian and Arabian wolves, 25 kg (55 lb).[7] Although rare, wolves weighing over 77 kg (177 lb) have been found in Alaska, Canada,[8] and the former Soviet Union.
